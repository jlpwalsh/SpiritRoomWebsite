Special Events

 

"The music these two women make dances across the prairie trailing feathers, blossoms and the laughter of geese"
- Athena Kildegaard
Prairie Renaisance Cultural Alliance

"The music of the Granary Girls is rooted in tradition and sen together with strong theads of feminism. They weave a blend of feminism that's all their own, homespun and true. When harmony singing is talked about, there is much attention and praise given to the notion of tight harmonies. The harmonies created by the Granary Girls are loose and flowing, rich and beautiful, the kind you thought you had to die to hear. Feminism with a beat!"
- Catherine Azora-Minda
Host of KFAI Women Folk Radio Program, MPLS 2002

"Songs that evolk the wide-open grandeur of the western prairie... I really appreciate the fact that so many of the songs are based around real places and people... about Border... Song Nice story behind that song. I can just kind of see that car going back and forth across the border"
- Tom May - River City Folk - Host & Producer

"The Granary Girls caught us up in the spirit of the stories and the music and brought us to our feet. We ended the night clapping and swaying and singing."
- AAUW Annual Convention
