Circle Of Friends

In conjunction with World Healing Day, the Spirit Room, 111 Broadway, will host an afternoon of meditation and reflection, Saturday, April 6 from 1:00 to 5:00 PM. The afternoon begins with a Mindfullness Meditation with Mary Struck, followed by a circular walking meditation from 2:00 to 4:00. A labyrinth will be placed in the center of the space and seven people will be given the opportunity to walk the labyrinth. Reservations to walk the labyrinth can be made by e-mail to spirit@ideaone.net . Participants are free to come and go during the group walking meditation. Labyrinths have been used to heighten spiritual awareness throughout history. The labyrinth to be used on Saturday is patterned after that at Chartes Cathedral in France. Participants walk to the center of the geometric pattern going inward spiritually, stopping at the center for reflection, prayer, meditation or contemplation and then return to the world. The participant then joins the group walking meditation. At 4:00 a Gratitude Ceremony will be led my Mary Struck based on that of Thich Nhat Hanh. Thich Nhat Hanh is a Vietnamese Buddhist monk who worked for peace in Viet Nam and was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize in 1967. For more information visit the Spirit Room web site World Healing Day, Saturday April 6, will be celebrated throughout the world. Celebrations begin in Australia, the earliest time zone and spread through six continents and 80 countries to remind us of the need to live in health, harmony and peace with one another and with our planet.
